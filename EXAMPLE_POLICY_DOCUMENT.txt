NATIONAL RENEWABLE ENERGY TRANSITION POLICY 2025-2050

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This policy outlines comprehensive strategies to transform our national energy sector, focusing on renewable energy adoption, grid modernization, and universal energy access. The policy aims to achieve 60% renewable energy share by 2035 and 85% by 2050, while reducing energy poverty from current 28% to below 10% by 2040.

CURRENT ENERGY SECTOR STATUS (2024)
- Total electricity generation: 185 TWh annually
- Renewable energy share: 22% (solar 8%, wind 10%, hydro 4%)
- Fossil fuel dependency: 78% (coal 45%, natural gas 28%, oil 5%)
- Electricity access rate: 72% (urban 89%, rural 58%)
- Energy poverty index: 28.3%
- CO2 emissions: 125 million metric tons
- Average electricity demand per capita: 1,450 kWh/year
- Average electricity demand per capita with access: 2,014 kWh/year
- Clean cooking access: 45% of population
- Peak demand: 8.5 GW
- Installed capacity: 12.3 GW (renewable: 2.7 GW, fossil: 9.6 GW)

POLICY TARGETS AND MILESTONES

Phase 1 (2025-2030):
- Increase renewable energy share to 35%
- Install 5 GW of new solar capacity
- Install 3 GW of new wind capacity
- Deploy 1.5 GW of battery storage systems
- Reduce CO2 emissions by 25%
- Increase electricity access to 85%
- Reduce energy poverty to 20%

Phase 2 (2031-2040):
- Achieve 60% renewable energy share
- Install additional 8 GW solar capacity
- Install additional 5 GW wind capacity
- Deploy 4 GW of battery storage
- Reduce CO2 emissions by 50% from 2024 baseline
- Achieve 95% electricity access
- Reduce energy poverty to 10%

Phase 3 (2041-2050):
- Achieve 85% renewable energy share
- Complete grid modernization
- Achieve 98% electricity access
- Reduce energy poverty to below 5%
- Achieve net-zero emissions in power sector

INVESTMENT REQUIREMENTS
- Total investment needed: $45 billion over 25 years
- Solar infrastructure: $18 billion
- Wind infrastructure: $12 billion
- Battery storage: $8 billion
- Grid modernization: $5 billion
- Transmission infrastructure: $2 billion

ANNUAL INVESTMENT BREAKDOWN
2025-2030: $1.8 billion/year
2031-2040: $2.2 billion/year
2041-2050: $1.5 billion/year

KEY POLICY ACTIONS

1. Renewable Energy Deployment
   - Establish feed-in tariff program: $0.12/kWh for solar, $0.10/kWh for wind
   - Provide tax incentives: 30% tax credit for renewable energy investments
   - Streamline permitting process: reduce approval time from 18 months to 6 months
   - Create renewable energy zones covering 15,000 km²

2. Grid Modernization
   - Upgrade transmission infrastructure: 8,000 km of new transmission lines
   - Implement smart grid technologies: 5 million smart meters by 2035
   - Enhance grid flexibility: increase inter-regional capacity by 40%
   - Deploy grid-scale storage: 4 GW by 2040

3. Energy Access Expansion
   - Rural electrification program: connect 12 million households by 2040
   - Mini-grid development: 3,000 mini-grids in remote areas
   - Off-grid solar: distribute 5 million solar home systems
   - Subsidized connection fees: reduce from $200 to $50 per household

4. Energy Efficiency
   - Building efficiency standards: reduce energy consumption by 30%
   - Appliance efficiency program: phase out inefficient appliances by 2030
   - Industrial efficiency: reduce industrial energy intensity by 25%

5. Clean Cooking Access
   - Current clean cooking access: 45%
   - Target: 80% by 2035, 95% by 2050
   - LPG distribution: expand to 8 million households
   - Biogas program: install 500,000 biogas plants
   - Clean cooking access target: 95% by 2050
